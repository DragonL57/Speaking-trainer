# Default Practice Scripts for Pronunciation Evaluation

## Beginner Level

### Basic Sentences
1. The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.
2. How are you doing today?
3. I would like a cup of coffee, please.
4. The weather is nice and sunny.
5. Can you help me with this problem?

### Common Phrases
1. Thank you very much for your help.
2. Excuse me, where is the nearest bank?
3. I'm sorry, I don't understand.
4. What time does the store close?
5. Nice to meet you!

## Intermediate Level

### Tongue Twisters
1. She sells seashells by the seashore.
2. Peter Piper picked a peck of pickled peppers.
3. How much wood would a woodchuck chuck if a woodchuck could chuck wood?
4. I scream, you scream, we all scream for ice cream!
5. Betty Botter bought some butter, but she said the butter's bitter.

### Phoneme-Rich Sentences
1. The thirty-three thieves thought that they thrilled the throne throughout Thursday.
2. Fred fed Ted bread and Ted fed Fred bread.
3. A proper copper coffee pot.
4. The rain in Spain stays mainly in the plain.
5. Six sticky skeletons.

## Advanced Level

### Complex Sentences
1. Despite the inclement weather, the determined hikers continued their arduous journey through the mountainous terrain.
2. The pharmaceutical company's innovative research methodology yielded unprecedented results in clinical trials.
3. Contemporary literature often explores themes of existential crisis and societal transformation.
4. The archaeological excavation revealed artifacts dating back to the Mesozoic era.
5. Quantum mechanics challenges our fundamental understanding of reality at the subatomic level.

### Professional Contexts
1. I'd like to schedule a meeting to discuss the quarterly financial projections.
2. The implementation of our new software system will significantly improve operational efficiency.
3. Could you please elaborate on your methodology for data analysis?
4. Our strategic initiative focuses on sustainable development and corporate social responsibility.
5. The presentation will cover market trends, competitive analysis, and growth opportunities.

## Prosody Practice

### Questions and Statements
1. Are you coming to the party tonight? (Rising intonation)
2. I can't believe you did that! (Emphasis and emotion)
3. Would you like coffee, tea, or juice? (List intonation)
4. That's absolutely wonderful news! (Excitement)
5. I'm not sure if I understand correctly. (Uncertainty)

### Emphasis Practice
1. I didn't say he stole the money. (Emphasis on "I")
2. I didn't say he stole the money. (Emphasis on "say")
3. I didn't say he stole the money. (Emphasis on "he")
4. I didn't say he stole the money. (Emphasis on "stole")
5. I didn't say he stole the money. (Emphasis on "money")
